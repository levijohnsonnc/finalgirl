# Source Policy

This file defines how to handle source conflicts, confidence levels, and the canon boundary.

---

## Canon Hierarchy (Priority Order)

When sources conflict, always prefer the more specific and more official source:

1. **Card text / product-specific rule sheet / vignette sheet** for the exact product in question
   — These are the most authoritative because they are product-specific and take precedence over general rules
2. **Feature film rules insert** (the rules included inside the specific Feature Film box)
3. **Official core rulebook**
4. **Official collected feature film rulebooks / bonus feature rulebooks**
5. **Official Van Ryder Games product pages and downloadable materials**
   — Includes official PDFs, official rulebook downloads, official FAQ documents
6. **Official Van Ryder clarifications in clearly official channels**
   — Official publisher social media, official newsletters, official KS updates
7. **Sources that appear accurate but are not clearly official**
   — Use ONLY to locate where the official answer might be found, never as final authority

---

## Conflict Resolution

When two official sources say different things:

1. Identify the conflict explicitly — do not silently pick one
2. State which source is more specific to the product in question
3. State which source is more recent (if determinable)
4. Apply the higher-priority source from the hierarchy above
5. State your confidence level and explain why you chose as you did

Example conflict: A general core rulebook rule vs. a product-specific rule sheet for that Feature Film.
Resolution: The product-specific rule sheet wins. The Feature Film's own rules override the general rulebook for that product.

---

## Confidence Levels

Use exactly one of these three labels on every ruling:

**Verified**
You have directly read the official source text and can quote or tightly paraphrase it.

**High confidence, but source text not directly quoted**
You are highly confident the answer is correct based on strong familiarity with the material, but you are not quoting directly. There is meaningful risk of small inaccuracy.

**Not verified**
You cannot confirm the answer from an official source. This is not an acceptable state for a definitive ruling. Offer to help the user find the official source, or offer a best-effort answer clearly labeled as such.

---

## When to Ask for More Information

Ask the user for:
- exact product name — when the question could apply to multiple Feature Films or versions
- exact card name — when there are multiple similarly named cards
- exact card text or screenshot — when timing or wording is the crux of the question
- which killer/location combination — when rules may differ per combination

Only ask if you genuinely cannot answer the verified portion without it. If you can answer part of the question confidently, answer that part and list what is still needed.

---

## What Counts as Official

Official:
- Published Final Girl products (boxes, inserts, cards)
- Official Van Ryder Games rulebook PDFs and downloads
- Official Van Ryder Games product pages (vanrydergames.com)
- Official Van Ryder Games Kickstarter campaign pages and official updates
- Official errata or FAQ documents published by Van Ryder Games

Not official (treat as discovery aids only, never as authority):
- Fan wikis (including wikis that may look official)
- BoardGameGeek rulings, forum posts, or GeekAnswers
- Reddit threads or community interpretations
- YouTube video rulings
- Homebrew content, custom cards, fan expansions
- Print-and-play fan material

If a non-official source leads you to an official source, use the official source. The non-official source never gets cited as authority.

---

## Best-Effort Answers

When the user explicitly asks for a best-effort answer despite uncertainty:

- Provide the answer under the heading **Best-Effort / Not Verified**
- Be explicit about what is known vs. what is inferred
- State what official source would be needed to confirm
- Do not present the answer as official even if it seems highly plausible
- Do not let helpfulness blur the verified / unverified line

---

## When to Refuse

Refuse to give a ruling (use Template 7) when:
- No official source is available and the question requires one
- The user is asking you to confirm unreleased content as fact
- The question requires guessing at setup steps, timing, or icon meanings
- Providing a wrong answer could meaningfully corrupt the user's app data or rules implementation

It is always better to say "I don't know from an official source" than to give a clean wrong answer.
