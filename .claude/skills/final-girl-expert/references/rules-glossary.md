# Rules Glossary

This file defines recurring Final Girl game terms, timing concepts, and standard mechanical structures.

These definitions are derived from official Final Girl materials. Where a term has product-specific variations, those variations must be looked up in the relevant product's rules — do not assume universal behavior.

---

## Core Game Structure

**Final Girl**
The protagonist. The player controls the Final Girl as she attempts to survive and kill the killer.

**Killer**
The antagonist. Moves and acts according to the Killer's specific rules and cards from their Feature Film. Each killer has unique abilities, Dark Powers, and Finales.

**Feature Film**
A standalone expansion box containing one killer, one location, and one or more final girls, plus all associated cards and rules for that combination. Requires the Core Box to play.

**Location**
The environment where the game takes place. Each location has its own item deck, event deck, and setup rules. Part of a Feature Film.

**Core Box**
The base game box. Contains the shared components needed to play any Feature Film: the Final Girl board, tokens, dice, and the core rulebook.

---

## Item States and Zones

**Inbox**
Not a Final Girl term — do not use this framing in the app.

**Hand**
Cards held by the player that are currently available.

**Discard**
Cards that have been used and are no longer in play (unless retrieved by a specific effect).

**Deck**
A face-down stack of cards to be drawn from.

---

## Turn Structure Concepts

**Action**
The core unit of player activity on a turn. The Final Girl has a limited number of actions per turn (determined by the game state).

**Reaction / Interrupt**
An effect that occurs in response to another action or event, potentially outside normal turn order. Timing matters — always verify against the specific card text.

**Timing Window**
The moment at which a card, ability, or effect can legally be played. Final Girl uses specific timing language on cards. Do not infer timing from card name or theme — read the card text.

---

## Killer Mechanics

**Dark Power**
A special ability belonging to the killer. Each killer has a unique set of Dark Powers with distinct triggers and effects. Do not assume any Dark Power behaves like another killer's similarly named ability.

**Finale**
The end-game confrontation mechanic specific to each killer. Finales vary significantly between Feature Films. Contents and rules must be verified per product.

**Killer Movement**
How the killer moves through the location each turn. Movement rules are killer-specific and location-specific. Never generalize between killers.

**Attack**
When the killer strikes the Final Girl or a bystander. Timing of movement relative to attack is a common source of rules questions — always verify per the specific killer's rules.

---

## Survivor Mechanics

**Bystander**
A non-player character present in the location. Bystanders can be rescued, used as items, or killed by the killer depending on the Feature Film rules.

**Rescue**
The act of saving a bystander. Mechanics vary by Feature Film.

---

## Card Types

**Event Card**
A card drawn during the game that triggers a specific event at the location. Events are location-specific.

**Terror Card**
A card that increases tension or activates the killer. Terror mechanics are central to the game's escalation system.

**Item Card**
A card representing an item the Final Girl can find and use. Items are generally location-specific.

**Setup Card**
A card used during game setup to establish the initial state of the location, bystanders, or other elements.

---

## Sequencing and Timing Notes

Timing questions in Final Girl often hinge on:
1. Whether an effect triggers before or after a specific action
2. Whether a reaction can interrupt a sequence already in progress
3. Whether a card's effect applies during setup, during play, or only at specific moments

For any timing dispute:
- Read the exact card text first
- Check the product-specific rule sheet second
- Check the core rulebook third
- If still ambiguous, report the ambiguity rather than inventing a ruling

---

## Difficulty Concepts

**Difficulty Rating**
Some Feature Films carry an official difficulty rating. These are set by Van Ryder Games and should be recorded in the product catalog when verified. Do not assign difficulty ratings based on community consensus.

**Playstyle Notes**
Observations about whether a Feature Film plays aggressively, defensively, with tight resource management, etc. These should be labeled as interpretation, not official design intent, unless Van Ryder has explicitly stated them.

---

## Product Dependency Terms

**Standalone**
A product that can be played without any other product. (Note: In Final Girl, only the Core Box + a Feature Film together constitute a playable set. Individual Feature Films are NOT standalone — they require the Core Box.)

**Requires Core Box**
All Feature Films require the Core Box to play.

**Requires Feature Film**
Some products (vignettes, some bonus features) require a specific Feature Film in addition to the Core Box.

**Compatible With**
A product that can be used with multiple Feature Films or killers/locations.

---

## Glossary Maintenance Note

This glossary covers common terms. For product-specific mechanics, consult the relevant Feature Film's rules insert or the product-catalog.md file. Terms with variations across products should always be verified against the specific product rather than this general glossary.
